I am an economics undergraduate at the University of Oregon. My research interests lie in international trade, historical tariff policy, and global value chains. I am working toward PhD-track empirical research in these areas.

My current project examines the welfare and sectoral price effects of the McKinley Tariff Act of 1890 using a multi-sector input-output framework calibrated to production data from the 1890 Census of Manufacturing.

I am interested more broadly in the political economy of trade protection, the long-run consequences of industrial policy, and the use of historical data to identify causal mechanisms in trade.
