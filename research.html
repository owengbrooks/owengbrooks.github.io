Trade Diversion and the McKinley Tariff of 1890: Evidence from Digitized Customs Records

In progress · 2025

I construct a panel of bilateral trade flows from digitized U.S. customs records spanning 1883–1903 and estimate the structural gravity model of Eaton and Kortum (2002) to recover the trade and welfare consequences of the McKinley Act. Preliminary results suggest substantial diversion away from European suppliers toward domestic production, concentrated in metal manufactures and woolen goods.
