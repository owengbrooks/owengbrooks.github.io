The McKinley Tariff of 1890: Input-Output Amplification and Sectoral Reallocation

In progress · 2025

I quantify the welfare and sectoral price effects of the McKinley Tariff following Caliendo and Parro (2015) multi-sector input-output hat algebra. Using a multi-sector input-output table for intermediate cost shares and production data from the 1890 Census of Manufacturing, I calibrate the model to 21 sectors. Predicated upon a small open economy assumption, the aggregate welfare effect was +0.304%. This finding reflects the heterogeneous tariff schedule that has not been explored in general equilibrium. The act raised protection for manufactured goods while significantly cutting tariffs on sugar, chemicals, and agriculture. Input-output linkages reveal the knock-on effects of the tariff on upstream industries that were on the duty-free list.
